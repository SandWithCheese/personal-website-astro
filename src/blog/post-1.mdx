# My first MDX post

This opening paragraph is intentionally a little longer than usual. It should
show how normal body text feels when it has room to breathe, how links like
[Astro](https://astro.build) sit inside a sentence, and whether the first block
of writing feels inviting enough to keep reading.

The goal of this page is not to say anything important. It is here to stress
the styling: paragraphs, headings, lists, quotes, code, separators, images, and
other small pieces that tend to look fine in isolation but weird when they are
all sitting together.

## A Section Heading

Good article styling should make hierarchy obvious without shouting. A second
level heading should feel like a clear new room in the essay, not a billboard.

Here is a paragraph with **bold emphasis**, _italic emphasis_, and `inline code`.
The inline code should feel distinct, but it should not punch a hole through the
line height.

## A Longer Passage

There is a particular kind of writing that is less about announcing conclusions
and more about keeping a record of attention. It can be technical one day,
personal the next, and somewhere in between most of the time. That kind of
writing needs a layout that does not over-explain itself.

It should make room for uncertainty. It should let a small note feel complete,
but also let a longer essay stretch out without tiring the reader. The page does
not need to impress anyone at first glance. It needs to feel steady after ten
minutes.

That is the bar for this test content.
